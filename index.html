The Ashray Journey

A Joyful Beginning

As the popular Hindi song 'Phir Bhi Dil Hai Hindustani' starts to play, four children wearing army uniform costumes begin to dance, with big smiles. Their happiness, sincerity, and innocence shine through. And this is the joy they have found at Ashray - a safe and secure home for children with disabilities where they're looked after with love and respect. That dance was during our Annual Day program, and it's always such a special day for all of us.

The Heart Behind Ashray

"I believe that every child is unique and has different needs and talents, but that doesn't make them a burden! But sadly, many children with physical and mental disabilities are abandoned by their families as they are often considered difficult to look after. This breaks my heart because it's not the child's fault at all. That's why I built Ashray, to give these children a place to call home and a family to call their own." - Anil Joseph, Founder, Integrated Institute of Rehabilitation for the Disabled They Keep Waiting For Their Families To Show Up.

Anil's Incredible Journey

After completing his Diploma in Special Education in 2002, Anil started working for an NGO in Agra as a special educator. During one of his visits to a clinic, he came across a mother and her baby who had a disability. Her husband was threatening to leave her because of the baby's condition, and she was absolutely helpless. Anil knew he had to do something - he took the baby in, and that was the beginning of his incredible journey of giving children with physical and mental disabilities a better life .In 2005, he started the Integrated Institute of Rehabilitation for the Disabled and built a shelter home called Ashray in Agra. Today, he looks after over 72 children.

The Call for Support

Children with physical and mental disabilities often require special attention, and since so many of them have various skills and needs, Anil needs enough space to ensure all of them learn, grow, and play. Unfortunately, the current shelter home is too small. Anil is building a bigger one nearby but has only managed to build the hostels so far. He needs your support to build the classrooms. "It's so important for us to give them the space they deserve, but I just can't do it on my own. I need your help to get construction materials so that I can give these children a bigger, better classroom. I also need help getting a mini ambulance. We have to keep taking them for check-ups, treatments, and physio, and a small ambulance will make all the difference."

About the NGO

Started by Anil Joseph in 2005, Ashray is a shelter home in Agra for children with physical and mental disabilities where their food, shelter, medical treatment, and educational needs are taken care of. Currently, they have 72 children who rely on them for their every need.
